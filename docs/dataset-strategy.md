# Dataset Audit And Strategy

## Reproducible Audit

Audit date: June 17, 2026.

The local corpus audit is generated by code, not maintained manually. Re-run it
whenever `../Pretraining` changes:

```bash
python scripts/analyze_pretraining_dataset.py \
  --pretraining-root ../Pretraining \
  --dataset-list Dataset.yml \
  --output-dir reports/pretraining_dataset_audit
```

Generated artifacts:

- `reports/pretraining_dataset_audit/pretraining_dataset_audit.md`
- `reports/pretraining_dataset_audit/pretraining_dataset_audit.json`
- `reports/pretraining_dataset_audit/pretraining_dataset_audit.csv`

The scanner reads ZIP central directories without extraction. It counts images
by extension, samples a small number of images for mode and band inspection, and
flags containers such as HDF5 without expanding their internal arrays.

## Current Local Corpus

The sibling `Pretraining/` directory currently contains a diverse set of local ZIP datasets and unzipped directories.

| Metric | Value |
| --- | ---: |
| Total Images Counted | ~1.87 Million |
| Total Zips/Unzipped Folders | > 70 |

### Major Components (~1.87M Total Images)
- **General Species Classification:** Massive representation from datasets like `plantnet_300K-018` (~306K), `GeoPlant` (~188K), and `Plants leafs Dataset` (~190K).
- **Plant Diseases & Pathology:** Very strong focus here. Includes `Plant Disease Expert` (~200K), `PlantVillage` (~162K), `PlantifyDr` (~125K), and crop-specific disease datasets for Wheat, Rice, Cotton, Chili, and Pumpkin.
- **Weed Detection:** Strong presence with `OPPD` (unzipped folders like `VERAR`, `PAPRH`, `CAPBP`, etc., which are EPPO plant codes), `Weed Detection in Soybean Crops`, `DeepWeeds`, and `CottonWeedDet3`.
- **Specialty/Other:** `Toxic Plant Classification`, `Indian Medicinal Plants`, `House Plant Species`, and `Edible wild plants`.
- **Seedlings/Growth Stages:** `V2 Plant Seedlings` and `pretraining_seedlings` subsets.

## Current Coverage

Strengths:

- Massive RGB image volume (~1.87 million).
- Strong plant species, leaf disease, and weed classification coverage.
- Useful seedling, crop type, rice, chili, medicinal, edible, and toxic plant subsets.
- Class-folder structure is easy to use for SSL pretraining and downstream classification.

Major gaps:

- **Aerial/UAV (Drone) and Satellite Imagery:** Most data consists of close-up, ground-level RGB images of leaves or single plants. Lack of field-level remote sensing.
- **Multispectral / Hyperspectral Images:** No explicit representation of Near-Infrared (NIR) or multispectral data needed to calculate indices like NDVI.
- **Pests and Insects:** Strong on pathogens, weak on physical pests (insects/bugs) on crops.
- **Temporal/Time-Series Data:** Lack of images showing the same crop/field over its growth cycle.
- **Harvest/Yield Data:** Images of fruits, vegetables, or grains during harvest, which are useful for yield estimation or fruit counting tasks.
- Duplicate and near-duplicate analysis still needs to be performed before a publication-scale run.
- License, citation, version, and download-date metadata should be frozen per source.

## Priority Additions

1. Real multispectral and georeferenced agricultural imagery with documented bands. 
2. Sentinel-2, Landsat, UAV, and field-scale GeoTIFF tiles with RGB, NIR, red-edge, and metadata.
3. Pest/insect focused datasets (e.g., IP102).
4. Temporal tracking of crops and harvest/yield focused datasets.
5. External datasets reserved exclusively for transfer evaluation.

## Target Journals Strategy

### CVPR Target (Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition)
- Focus on methodological novelty, massive scale, and pushing the SOTA.
- **Novelty in SSL:** Adapt architecture for agriculture (e.g., handling extreme visual similarity between weeds/crops, plant-morphology aware masking in MIM).
- **Scale and Benchmarking:** Build a rigorous "Ag-Benchmark" suite. Empirically prove the Ag-Foundation model outperforms generic foundation models (DINOv2, CLIP).

### CompAG Target (Computers and Electronics in Agriculture)
- Focus on practical, robust applications to solve real-world agricultural problems.
- **Robustness:** Demonstrate few-shot learning capabilities. Show fine-tuning requires significantly less annotated data.
- **Cross-Domain Generalization:** Prove the model generalizes across geographic regions, lighting, and sensor types.

## Curation Requirements

Before a publication-scale run, create a frozen manifest with:

- source name and canonical URL
- download date and version
- license and redistribution status
- file count, byte size, and format distribution
- channel names and wavelength metadata
- spatial resolution and CRS where applicable
- country, farm, season, crop, and sensor when known
- duplicate and near-duplicate clusters
- train/validation/test assignment
- known label noise and exclusions

## Split Policy

The current loader offers group-disjoint splitting, but the group definition must   
be curated. For final evaluation, prefer:

- farm-held-out
- geography-held-out
- season-held-out
- sensor-held-out
- source-dataset-held-out
- crop-held-out

PlantNet's folder structure includes split and species in the group path. It        
must be remapped if the scientific split should operate at species, observation,    
or geography level.

## Publication Positioning

The defensible contribution is not "many datasets combined." It is:

- a provenance-aware, deduplicated, multimodal agricultural corpus
- sensor-adaptive continual pretraining from official ImageNet ViTs
- controlled comparison of reconstruction and self-distillation objectives
- broad transfer under realistic domain shift
- transparent data and evaluation governance
